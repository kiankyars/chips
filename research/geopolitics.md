## The chokepoints in numbers
- **Lithography**: ASML (Netherlands) = **100%** of EUV — sole supplier on Earth. [1]
- **Leading-edge logic**: TSMC = **72.3%** of ALL foundry revenue (Q1 2026) [2];
  **~90%** of the world's most advanced chips [3]. Taiwan the island = **92%** of
  sub-10nm fabrication capacity (SIA/BCG) [4]; ~**70%** of sub-7nm capacity (2022,
  USITC) [5].
- **EDA (chip-design software)**: Synopsys **31%** + Cadence **30%** + Siemens EDA
  **13%** = **>90%** of global market; two of three US, one German-owned-US-run. [6]
- **Materials**: Japanese firms (JSR, Tokyo Ohka, Shin-Etsu…) = **>90%** of high-end
  photoresist incl. the EUV resists nothing advanced prints without. [7]
- **HBM (AI memory)**: SK hynix **62%** + Samsung **17%** ≈ **~80% Korea** (Q2 2025);
  SK hynix still **56.4%** in Q1 2026; Micron (US) the rest. [8][9]
- **Equipment beyond litho**: US trio Applied Materials / Lam / KLA dominate deposition,
  etch, metrology — the legal hook for US extraterritorial controls.
- Punchline: **no nation controls the whole chain** — a leading-edge chip needs US design
  software + US/JP/NL equipment + Japanese chemicals + Taiwanese fabrication + Korean
  memory. Everyone has a hand on someone else's windpipe.

## Export-control timeline (the story arc)
**Act 1 — one company (2019–2020)**
- **May 2019** — Huawei added to the Entity List: US suppliers need licenses. First time
  the supply chain is weaponized against a single company. [10]
- **May 2020** — Foreign Direct Product Rule extended: any chip made *anywhere* with US
  tools is covered → TSMC stops taking Huawei orders (final cutoff Sept 2020). Huawei's
  HiSilicon Kirin line dies at its peak. [10]

**Act 2 — a whole country (2022–2024)**
- **Oct 7, 2022** — the sweeping controls: advanced-chip *and* chipmaking-equipment
  exports to all of China restricted; even US persons barred from supporting Chinese
  advanced fabs. Shift from company-targeting to country-wide capability denial. [10]
- **Oct 17, 2023** — tightening: performance-density rules kill the made-for-China
  A800/H800 loophole chips; more equipment covered; Nvidia designs the H20 as the new
  compliant chip. [11]
- **Dec 2, 2024** — third major round: HBM exports controlled, **24** new equipment
  categories + **3** software categories, **140** entities added (tool makers, fabs,
  investment vehicles). [12][13]

**Act 3 — the deal era (2025)**
- **Jan 15, 2025** — Biden's parting shot: the **AI Diffusion Rule** (global tiered caps
  on AI-chip exports). Never takes effect. [14]
- **Apr 9, 2025** — Trump admin bans the H20 to China (license requirement, indefinite);
  Nvidia announces up to **$5.5B** charge — booked **$4.5B** in Q1. [15][16]
- **May 13, 2025** — AI Diffusion Rule **rescinded** two days before effect ("overly
  bureaucratic… stifled American innovation"); replaced by guidance, incl. that using
  Huawei Ascend chips anywhere may violate US export rules. [14]
- **Jul–Aug 2025** — the reversal-for-revenue: H20 sales to China re-allowed; Nvidia &
  AMD agree to pay the US government **15%** of China chip revenue (H20 / MI308) for
  licenses — negotiated down from Trump's opening ask of 20%. Never codified as a
  regulation. [17]
- **Aug 2025 backfire** — Beijing discourages H20 purchases (security reviews, state-media
  "not safe" campaign); Nvidia reportedly halts H20 production. [18]
- **Aug 22, 2025** — US converts Intel's CHIPS grants into a **9.9% equity stake** (see
  CHIPS Act section). [39]
- **Aug 29, 2025** — BIS revokes **VEU** status for Samsung-China, SK hynix-China, Intel
  Dalian (TSMC Nanjing loses it too), effective **Dec 31, 2025**: every equipment upgrade
  in their China fabs now needs case-by-case licenses; by year-end an annual-review
  arrangement softens the blow. [19][20]
- **Sep 29, 2025** — the **Affiliates Rule**: any entity ≥50%-owned by Entity-List/MEU
  parties is automatically covered — closes the subsidiary-shell loophole, mirrors
  OFAC's 50% rule. [21]
- **Oct–Nov 2025** — escalation to truce: China's sweeping rare-earth controls (Oct 9) →
  **Trump–Xi Busan summit (Oct 30)**: 1-year rare-earth pause, fentanyl tariff 20%→10%
  (overall ~47%), port-fee suspension. [22]

**Act 4 — managed trade (H1 2026)**
- **Jan 15, 2026** — the grand rebalance, three moves in one day:
  1. **Section 232 proclamation**: **25% tariff** on advanced semiconductors above
     defined performance thresholds (H200/MI325X class) — with carve-outs for US data
     centers, startups, R&D; broader "phase 2" tariffs held as negotiating leverage;
     Commerce report on data-center chip market due **Jul 1, 2026**. [23][24]
  2. **BIS license-review flip**: exports of H200-class chips to China move from
     presumption-of-denial to **case-by-case** — if the exporter certifies sufficient US
     supply, no diverted foundry capacity, security procedures, third-party US testing.
     The H200 — a chip banned in 2023 — becomes conditionally exportable. [25][26]
  3. **US–Taiwan trade deal** (see Taiwan section). [51]
- **Feb 2026** — Nvidia targets first H200 China shipments mid-Feb; as of late Feb it has
  booked **zero** revenue on approved sales — Chinese buyers hesitant, Beijing pressure,
  domestic rivals ramping. [27][28]
- **May 2026** — **Trump–Xi Beijing summit**: chip export controls reportedly not even
  discussed at the bilateral; around it, Washington clears H200 sales to several major
  Chinese tech firms. Rare-earth truce extension left unresolved. [29]
- **Jun 1, 2026** — Commerce affirms AI-chip restrictions follow the *parent*: Chinese
  companies' subsidiaries **outside China** are covered (closing the offshore-datacenter
  loophole). [30]
- **Jun 8, 2026** — Pentagon adds **65** entities to the 1260H "Chinese military
  companies" list — including **Alibaba, Baidu, BYD**, WuXi AppTec, Unitree; Pentagon
  procurement ban effective Jun 30. Diplomatic thaw takes a hit. [31]
- **Jun 22, 2026** — China retaliates (see counters). [32]
- Ongoing H1 2026 mood: administration soft-pedals controls to protect the trade truce;
  Congress pushes back, seeking statutory control over export licensing; Nvidia's
  made-for-China **B30A** awaits US green light. <!-- VERIFY: B30A approval status at record date --> [33][34]

## China's counters
- **Jul/Aug 2023** — export licensing on **gallium & germanium** (China ≈ 98% / 91% of
  global supply <!-- VERIFY: exact supply shares -->) — response to litho controls. [35]
- **Dec 3, 2024** — outright **ban** on gallium, germanium, antimony, superhard materials
  to the US — announced ~24h after the US HBM rules. First true tit-for-tat. [36]
- **Oct 9, 2025** — the big one: extraterritorial rare-earth controls modeled on the US
  FDPR — foreign-made goods containing China-origin rare-earth content require Beijing's
  license <!-- VERIFY: 0.1% de-minimis threshold detail -->. Panic → Busan truce: controls
  paused 1 year to **Nov 10, 2026**; mineral ban to US suspended to **Nov 27, 2026**. The
  leverage is parked, not dismantled. [22][37]
- **Jun 2026 escalation** — 10 US firms added to China's export-control list (incl. rare-earth
  miners **MP Materials, USA Rare Earth** — controls aimed at the companies meant to fix US
  dependence) in retaliation for the 1260H additions; MOFCOM Announcement No. 26 (effective
  **Jul 1, 2026**) adds a violation-reporting/whistleblower regime. [32][38]
- **The Nexperia crisis (the legacy-chip war)**:
  - Late Sep 2025 — Dutch government seizes control of Nexperia (Dutch chipmaker owned by
    China's Wingtech) under the Goods Availability Act; Chinese CEO removed, fearing tech
    transfer out of Europe. [40]
  - **Oct 4, 2025** — China blocks exports from Nexperia's Dongguan packaging plant
    (**>50 billion chips/yr**) → global auto industry weeks from line stoppages over
    cents-priced transistors; some production cuts. [41] <!-- VERIFY: which automakers actually cut output (Honda NA widely reported) -->
  - Partial de-escalation: China exempts civilian-use exports (Nov 2025); Dutch order
    suspended <!-- VERIFY: exact Dutch suspension date, ~Nov 19, 2025 -->; Nexperia China
    declares operational independence, sources domestic wafers. [41]
  - 2026 status: **unresolved**. Wingtech files investment-treaty arbitration for
    **>$8B** (May 2026) plus a €1B Dutch suit; Jul 8, 2026 — Dutch trade minister in
    Beijing says both sides cooperating "extremely well" to move past it (also lobbying
    on ASML's behalf). A real settlement needs Nexperia-Europe ↔ Nexperia-China terms. [40][42][43]
- **Antitrust as a weapon**: SAMR finds Nvidia violated the anti-monopoly law (Mellanox
  conditions) Sep 15, 2025 — probe left open as leverage; Qualcomm probe opened Oct 10,
  2025 (Autotalks deal; China ≈ 46% of Qualcomm revenue). Both still unresolved after the
  May 2026 summit — dangling concessions. [44][45][46]
- **Market-access denial**: Nov 2025 directive — state-funded data centers must use
  **domestic AI chips only**; projects <30% complete must rip out foreign chips. >$100B
  of state-funded AI-DC projects affected. Even the compliant H20 is out. [47]
- Customs crackdown on smuggled Nvidia chips + special port fees on US ships (Oct 2025). [46]

## China's domestic push (honest assessment)
- **Logic — SMIC**: 7nm (N+2) shipping since 2023 (Mate 60's Kirin 9000S); 5nm pilot via
  DUV multi-patterning (SAQP). Reported yields **~20–40%**, wafer costs far above TSMC —
  SMIC prices 7/5nm at a **40–50% premium** to TSMC equivalents. Physics tax: every node
  without EUV multiplies patterning steps, cost, defects. [48][49]
- **Huawei Ascend**: 910C target **~600k units in 2026** (2× 2025); up to **1.6M dies**
  across the Ascend line; Ascend 920 unveiled, 950PR due Q1 2026, 920C claimed on SMIC
  "6nm" <!-- VERIFY: SMIC 6nm claim is unconfirmed marketing -->. **CloudMatrix 384**
  (Apr 2025): 384×910C in 16 racks — beats an Nvidia GB200 NVL72 system on aggregate
  compute by brute force (more chips, ~4× the power). [50][51][52]
- **The real bottleneck is HBM**: CXMT projected to make ~2M HBM stacks in 2026 → enough
  for only **~250–300k** 910C-class packages without stockpiled foreign HBM. Dies aren't
  the constraint; memory is. [52]
- Huawei reportedly building its own fab network (Shenzhen, via proxies) targeting 7nm
  commercial scale. <!-- VERIFY: independent confirmation of Huawei-fab 7nm timeline -->
- **Lithography**:
  - SMIC testing a domestic immersion DUV scanner from a SiCarrier-affiliated project
    (Yuliangsheng) since Sep 2025; production insertion targeted **2027**; sub-10nm-capable
    domestic scanners realistically **not before ~2030**. [53][54]
  - SMEE reality check: its headline Dec 2025 litho contract turned out to be a **KrF
    248nm dry tool (110nm resolution)** — not the claimed 28nm immersion class. [55]
  - **EUV**: Reuters (Dec 2025) — prototype EUV machine secretly built in Shenzhen
    (completed early 2025, in testing); Huawei + SiCarrier coordinating **3,000+**
    researchers; Harbin Institute of Technology light source (LDP route) at
    **100–150W** vs the 250W ASML reached in **2017**. Beijing targets 2028 production;
    2030 more realistic. Signal: the moat is measured in years, not decades. [56][57]
    <!-- VERIFY: wattage range + 3,000-researcher figure against the original Reuters
    piece — consistently repeated across TechPowerUp/wccftech/Asia Times/The Diplomat,
    but the primary article was blocked from direct fetch -->
    Scale hedge: a prototype is not a production tool — ASML went first EUV prototype
    (2006) → volume-worthy scanners (~2017), a decade, with Zeiss optics China lacks.
- **Memory**: CXMT ≈ **8%** of global DRAM (Q1 2026), HBM3 targeted end-2026 (60k wpm,
  ~20% of its capacity), HBM3E 2027 — **3–4 years behind** Korea; YMTC at high-layer NAND
  (Xtacking hybrid bonding), new DRAM subsidiary (Sep 2025), teaming with CXMT on HBM
  packaging. [58][59][60][61]
- **EDA**: Empyrean = China's biggest domestic player at only **~10–12%** of the Chinese
  EDA market; big-three US/German firms ≈ 80% of China market but falling (Synopsys China
  sales −28% YoY H1 FY2025); the May–Jul 2025 US EDA export-control episode (imposed,
  then lifted) told every Chinese designer to de-risk. China EDA self-sufficiency ≈ >10%. [6][62][63]
- **RISC-V**: Beijing policy push for the open ISA as the escape hatch from Arm/x86
  licensing risk (national guidance 2025; XiangShan open-source cores, Alibaba XuanTie).
  <!-- VERIFY: March 2025 eight-ministry RISC-V guidance details -->
- **The gap, called honestly**: China can now *design* world-class AI chips and print
  7nm-class silicon in volume — what it can't yet do is print them *economically* (no
  EUV), stack the memory (HBM 3–4 yrs behind), or replace the tool/EDA/materials stack
  end-to-end. Consensus gap at leading edge ≈ **5± years**, closing faster than the 2022
  rule-writers assumed; export controls raised China's costs but also created a protected
  home market with unlimited state capital. Legacy nodes are the flip side: China's share
  of mature-node capacity is climbing toward ~⅓ globally. <!-- VERIFY: mature-node share projection (TrendForce ~30%+ by 2027) -->

## CHIPS Act → the equity era (US reshoring)
- **CHIPS & Science Act (Aug 2022)**: **$52.7B** total — $39B manufacturing incentives,
  $13.2B R&D/workforce, $500M supply-chain security — plus a **25%** investment tax
  credit (~$24B est.). [64]
- **Aug 22, 2025 — the pivot from grants to ownership**: US converts Intel's remaining
  CHIPS grants + Secure Enclave money into **433.3M shares at $20.47 = $8.9B ≈ 9.9%**
  of Intel — largest shareholder; plus 5-year warrant for another 5% at $20 if Intel
  ceases to own ≥51% of its foundry. [39][65]
- Sovereign-fund direction: officials frame such stakes as seeds of a US sovereign wealth
  fund; DoD took a preferred stake in MP Materials (Jul 2025). <!-- VERIFY: SWF framing quotes (Bessent), MP Materials stake ~15% -->
- **TSMC Arizona**: **$165B** total (Mar 4, 2025 announcement: +$100B on top of $65B) —
  six fabs, two advanced-packaging plants, R&D center; largest single FDI in US history.
  Fab 1 in 4nm volume production; yields reported to rival Taiwan <!-- VERIFY: ">92% yield" claim is from weak source -->;
  fab 3 (A16-class) accelerated toward 2029. [66][67]
- **Intel**: 18A (1.8nm-class) high-volume manufacturing at Fab 52, Arizona — most
  advanced node ever run on US soil. [68]
- **Samsung Taylor, TX**: ~$37B committed; unlocked by Tesla's **$16.5B** AI6 foundry
  deal (Jul 2025); 2nm-class production ramping 2026. <!-- VERIFY: current Taylor total and ramp date --> [68]
- **Micron**: **$200B** US plan (Jun 2025: ~$150B fabs — Idaho, New York, Virginia — +
  $50B R&D; $6.4B CHIPS grants), later raised to **$250B**. Only US-based memory maker. [69][70]
- **Scoreboard**: US leading-edge (sub-10nm) logic capacity **0% in 2022 → projected 28%
  by 2032**; US fab capacity to triple (+203%, fastest in world); ~$450B+ private
  chip investment announced since CHIPS. [71]
- Counterweight: Section 232 tariffs raise US AI-datacenter build costs — ITIF and
  others argue the tariff undermines the buildout it's meant to protect. [72]

## Taiwan
- The concentration: **92%** of sub-10nm capacity [4]; TSMC ≈ **90%** of most advanced
  chips [3]; N2 sold out through Q2 2027 [2].
- **War cost**: Bloomberg Economics — Taiwan war ≈ **$10T ≈ 10% of global GDP** (2024
  estimate); Feb 2026 update: **$10.6T / 9.6% of global GDP in year one** — bigger than
  COVID or the 2008 crisis. Blockade-only scenario ≈ $5T. [73][74] <!-- VERIFY: blockade $5T figure from original 2024 report -->
- **Silicon shield, argued both ways**:
  - *Shield*: the world can't afford to let Taiwan burn; US defense commitment is
    underwritten by TSMC dependence.
  - *Target/erosion*: the fabs make Taiwan worth seizing or strangling; and every fab
    TSMC builds in Arizona thins the shield — if the US gets its chips at home, what
    exactly does Taipei hold? (Taiwanese opposition calls US pressure "exploitation.") [75][76]
- **The 50-50 fight**: Lutnick's proposal — half of US-consumed chips made in US, half
  Taiwan (Oct 2025). Taiwan's vice premier publicly rejects it. [76]
- **Jan 15, 2026 US–Taiwan trade deal**: Taiwanese firms to invest **≥$250B** in US
  capacity + Taipei guarantees **$250B** credit; US caps reciprocal tariffs at **15%**
  (from 20%); Lutnick's stated goal: move **40%** of Taiwan's chip supply chain to the
  US. Taipei's Feb response: 40% relocation is "impossible" — most advanced nodes stay
  home. [51][75][77]

## Other theaters
- **Japan**: Rapidus — 2nm national champion from a standing start (founded 2022);
  pilot line running since Apr 2025, first 2nm GAA transistors Jul 2025; mass production
  target **2027**; cumulative government support ≈ **¥2.35T (~$16B)**, June 2026 ¥150B
  round — and Tokyo, like Washington, shifting from subsidies toward **equity stakes**. [78][79][80]
  Japan's quiet leverage: the photoresist chokepoint — late-2025 reports of possible
  export curbs rattled China more than any US rule. [7]
- **EU**: 2022 Chips Act **€43B** aimed at 20% world share by 2030 — Court of Auditors:
  target out of reach (share <10%); Intel's Magdeburg fab cancelled. **Chips Act 2.0**
  (proposal expected ~mid-2026): pivot from luring fabs to demand-side + supply-crisis
  powers; formal review due Sep 2026. Europe's real cards remain ASML + Zeiss. [81][82]
- **Middle East**: AI-chip diplomacy as export-control policy. **Stargate UAE**
  (OpenAI/G42/Nvidia/Oracle, ~$30B, 5GW campus in Abu Dhabi): first **200MW live 2026**,
  Vera Rubin systems powering the first gigawatt from H2 2026. Saudi **Humain** buildout
  with Nvidia/AMD announced May 2025. <!-- VERIFY: Humain deployment status mid-2026 -->
  The bet: Gulf compute stays on the US stack rather than drifting to Huawei's. [83][84][85]

## Risks & scenarios (for the closing beat)
- **Truce timers**: rare-earth pause expires **Nov 10, 2026**; mineral-ban suspension
  **Nov 27, 2026** — renewal was left ambiguous at the May 2026 summit. [22][29][37]
- **Taiwan tail risk**: $10T+ year-one global hit; no scenario in which anyone "wins"
  economically. [74]
- **Bifurcation is happening, not pending**: two AI stacks (CUDA/Nvidia vs Ascend/CANN),
  two tool chains, incompatible standards — the world's most globalized industry
  splitting into blocs. [34]
- **Controls' half-life**: each round buys years, not permanence — Mate 60, CloudMatrix,
  the EUV prototype each arrived faster than consensus predicted. [48][52][56]
- **US self-inflicted wounds**: tariffs taxing the AI buildout [72]; policy whiplash
  (ban→license→tariff in 9 months) makes compliance a casino; Congress vs. executive
  fight over licensing power. [33]
- **Silicon-shield erosion**: reshoring success ↔ Taiwan's security leverage — the two
  US goals partially contradict. [75]
- **Legacy-chip flood**: China's mature-node overcapacity as the next trade fight
  (autos, industrial), previewed by Nexperia. <!-- VERIFY: EU/US legacy-chip probes status 2026 -->

## Fact ammo — lines that land
- One Dutch company is 100% of EUV; China's crash program — 3,000+ researchers across
  Huawei, SiCarrier and state institutes — has, after years, produced a prototype whose
  light source hits 100–150W… the power level ASML passed in **2017**. [1][56][57]
- Bloomberg's model of a Taiwan war: **$10.6 trillion gone in year one** — roughly 10%
  of world GDP, more than COVID and the 2008 crisis. [74]
- The United States government is now **Intel's largest shareholder**: 9.9% for $8.9B —
  paid for with the subsidy money Congress passed to *grant*, not to invest. [39][65]
- Nvidia wrote off **$4.5B in one quarter** when the H20 was banned — four months later
  the ban was traded away for **15% of its China revenue**. An export control became a
  toll booth. [16][17]
- Dec 2, 2024: US bans HBM to China. Dec 3, 2024: China bans gallium and germanium to the
  US. Retaliation now runs on a **24-hour clock**. [12][36]
- The Nexperia fight proved you can hold the global car industry hostage with chips that
  cost **pennies**: one Dutch government seizure, one Chinese export halt at a Dongguan
  plant that packages **50+ billion chips a year**. [40][41]
- SMIC will sell you a 7nm wafer — at a **40–50% premium** over TSMC. Export controls
  work like a tax, and right now China is paying it in silicon. [49]
